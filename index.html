Open AI summary for "Explain what are barcodes":

Barcodes are a type of optical machine-readable representation of data, which are used to store information about a product or item. They are typically printed on labels or tags and are scanned by a barcode reader. Barcodes are composed of a series of vertical lines of varying widths and spaces, which represent numbers and letters. The information encoded in the barcode is determined by the widths and spaces of the lines. The most common type of barcode is the Universal Product Code (UPC), which is used to identify products in stores. UPC barcodes are composed of 12 digits, which are divided into two parts. The first six digits are the manufacturer's code, while the last six digits are the product's code. The barcode also contains a check digit, which is used to verify the accuracy of the code.
